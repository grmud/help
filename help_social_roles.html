Summary: General Mechanics: Gameplay Mechanics: Social Monsters: Roles


When you create a monster, you must pick a role that you want to play. The role determines your social interaction within the game, by limiting the special abilities available to your monster, but also by provides additional specialization in an area of the game.

Selecting Two Roles (Social Monsters Only)

When you choose a role, you have the option to choose one or two roles for your character (wandering monsters are only allowed to choose one). If you limit your monster only to a single role, then you will also have access to a handful of special abilities that other "hybrid" monsters (monsters with two roles) will not. Hybrid monsters will have more special ability choices, but find themselves more constrained in their special ability selection by the special ability slot limitations.

When you choose the first role, you will also be forced to choose a subtype of that role. Subtypes of roles give special bonuses to monsters for certain activities allowing them to specialize further in their field (e.g. a Trapper gains bonuses for trap activities, or a Diplomat may have members from a different caste in his immediate grumble.

The Five Roles

  • Leader - A leader is the only role that can head up a clan, and are able to command grunts for guard, bodyguard and patrol activities.
  • Shaman - A shaman is trained in the art of healing, sacrifice, and blessing; he is essential to a clan and/or group that engages in any combat.
  • Builder - The builder role gives the player the option to help construct, layout and maintain the clan lair, and construct traps.
  • Warrior - Just like the name says, a warrior specializes in combat activities.
  • Magical - The magical role gives a monster access to magical special abilities. There are no sub-types for this role.

Note: Warrior and Magical are the only roles that will be available for Wandering Monsters and Wandering Monsters are not be able to choose a role subtype.
