Summary: General Mechanics: Corpse


When a creature (human or monster) dies, it will leave a corpse on the ground. These corpses may be looted, and items may be taken from them.

LOOTING: To loot a corpse, type 'loot <corpse>'. Keep in mind that there may be more than one corpse in the area. If there are multiple corpse in the area, they will be labelled by number (corpse, 2 corpse, 3 corpse). Looting an adventurer will cause you to take all resources from their corpse that you can carry. Looting another player will only allow you to take a set portion of their resources. The rest of the resources remain with the monster who died.

GETTING ITEMS: To get an item from a corpse, type 'get <item> from <corpse>'. As per above, there may be more than one corpse, so be careful. You will not be able to pick up items if your inventory is full or if they are too big for you to carry. Monsters who die will only leave a portion of their items behind in their corpse. Keep in mind that the amount of items left behind can be reduced by spending karma, so monsters may not leave behind any items at all.

DEALING WITH LOOT: Loot is the general term used for accumulations of resources. When resources are in a room, they are all joined together into a single pile of loot. Helpful commands for loot:

Drop: You may drop resources in a room. Syntax: 'drop # <resource type>'. Example: 'drop 50 gold'.

Pay: You may pay resources to another monster. Syntax: 'pay # <resource type> to <monster>'. Example: 'pay 50 gold to Bob'.
