Grumble

Some animals form herds, others create packs. When a group of monsters get together, they form a grumble. A grumble consists of a rag tag group of monsters that wish to "group" together to socialize. A grumble differs from a from a clan for several reasons. Monsters in a grumble do not have to be from the same caste, a grumble may contain wandering monsters (however infrequent that might happen), grumbles do not need to be headed up by a leader. However, grumbles do not gain the costs and benefits gained from being in a clan, such as founding a lair, paying upkeep costs or gaining bonuses from your masters. Grumbles only offer a private communication channel and a way to organize.

Some useful terms in managing grumbles:

  • Master -- The term that is used to refer to the monster above you in a grumble
  • Minion -- The term used to refer to a monster that is directly beneath you in a grumble
  • invite <character> -- Invites another monster to join your grumble
  • accept <character> -- If another monster invites you to join your grumble, type this command to accept its invitation.
  • decline <character> -- If another monster invites you to become its minion, you can decline its offer.
  • discharge <master or minion> -- Use this commands either to remove one a minion that you command, or you can discharge your master, removing yourself (and your minions) from your master's grumble.
  • grumble -- Use this command to chat with all the monsters within your grumble
